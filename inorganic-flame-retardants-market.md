Aluminum Trihydrate (ATH), Magnesium Hydroxide (MDH), Antimony Trioxide (ATO), Zinc Borate (ZB), and others are commonly used inorganic flame retardants. ATH is known for its high thermal stability and smoke suppression properties. MDH releases water vapor when exposed to high temperatures, suppressing flames. ATO acts as a synergist with other flame retardants to enhance their effectiveness. ZB functions by releasing water vapor and creating a protective layer. Other inorganic flame retardants offer specific properties suitable for different applications.</p></p>
