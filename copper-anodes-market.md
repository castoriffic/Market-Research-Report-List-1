<p><p>Copper anodes are commonly used in various industries, and two types are particularly prominent: oxygen-free copper anodes and phosphorous copper anodes. Oxygen-free copper anodes are made from high-purity copper with very low oxygen content, ensuring excellent electrical conductivity and resistance to corrosion. These anodes are widely used in electrical and electronic applications. On the other hand, phosphorous copper anodes are alloyed with phosphorous, which enhances their mechanical properties and resistance to stress corrosion cracking. This type of anode is commonly employed in the manufacturing of marine components and equipment.</p></p>
