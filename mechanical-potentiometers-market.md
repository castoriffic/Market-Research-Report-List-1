<p><p>Mechanical potentiometers are electronic components used to vary and control resistance in a circuit. There are two main types of mechanical potentiometers, namely Through Hole Potentiometers and Surface Mount Device (SMD) Potentiometers. Through Hole Potentiometers are mounted onto the circuit board using leads that pass through holes, while SMD Potentiometers are directly soldered onto the surface of the circuit board. Through Hole Potentiometers are commonly used in applications where durability and stability are essential, while SMD Potentiometers are more compact and suitable for space-constrained designs.</p></p>
