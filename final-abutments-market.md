<p><p>Final abutments are used in dental implant procedures to connect the implant fixture to the prosthetic crown. There are four main types of final abutments: titanium, steel, ceramic, and zirconia. Titanium abutments are widely used due to their biocompatibility and durability. Steel abutments offer strength and stability, making them suitable for posterior teeth. Ceramic abutments provide aesthetic advantages but are less durable. Zirconia abutments combine strength and aesthetics, making them a popular choice for front teeth. Each type of abutment has its own unique properties, catering to different patient needs.</p></p>
