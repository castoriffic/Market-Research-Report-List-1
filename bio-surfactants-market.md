<p><p>Bio-surfactants are categorized into three types: an-ionic surfactants, cationic surfactants, and amphoteric surfactants. An-ionic surfactants have negatively charged hydrophilic groups and are effective in reducing surface tension. Cationic surfactants have positively charged hydrophilic groups and are useful in forming stable emulsions. Amphoteric surfactants have both positive and negative charged hydrophilic groups and can function as both anionic or cationic surfactants depending on pH. Each type serves different purposes in various industries, including cosmetics, agriculture, food processing, and oil exploration.</p></p>
