<p><p>The orthopaedic imaging equipment market comprises various types of imaging systems used for diagnosing orthopaedic conditions. X-ray systems employ electromagnetic radiation to produce images of bones, joints, and surrounding tissues. CT scanners utilize X-rays to generate detailed cross-sectional images. MRI systems use strong magnets and radio waves to produce detailed images of soft tissues and joints. EOS imaging systems offer radiation-free imaging with 3D capabilities. Ultrasound orthopaedic imaging equipment uses sound waves to create images of muscles, tendons, and ligaments. Nuclear imaging systems employ radioactive substances to create images of bones and soft tissues.</p></p>
