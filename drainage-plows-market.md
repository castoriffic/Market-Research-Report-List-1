<p><p>Drainage plows are trenching machines that aid in the process of creating drainage systems. There are primarily three types of drainage plows in the market. Drag plows are designed to be attached to a tractor and pulled through the ground, creating a trench. Power plows, on the other hand, are self-propelled and have their own engine to drive them forward. These plows are generally more powerful and suitable for larger projects. Hand plows are manual tools that require physical effort to create the trench, making them suitable for smaller-scale or precision work.</p></p>
