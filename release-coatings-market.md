<p><ul><li>Solvent Addition Cure</li><li>Solventless Addition Cure</li><li>Emulsion Addition Cure</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Release coatings are used in various industries to prevent adhesion between two surfaces. Solvent Addition Cure coatings consist of a solvent that evaporates after application, leaving a thin film on the surface. Solventless Addition Cure coatings, on the other hand, do not require any solvents and instead cure by a chemical reaction or by exposure to UV light. Emulsion Addition Cure coatings are made by combining water and an emulsion to create a stable mixture. Other types of release coatings may include silicone-based coatings or coatings with specific additives tailored for a particular application.</p></p>
