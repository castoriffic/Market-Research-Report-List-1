<p><p>Carbon capture and storage (CCS) refers to the process of capturing carbon dioxide (CO2) emitted from industrial processes and storing it underground to mitigate climate change. The market can be divided into three types: pre-combustion, oxy-combustion, and post-combustion. Pre-combustion involves capturing CO2 before it's burned as fuel, while oxy-combustion involves burning fuel in an oxygen-rich environment to produce a concentrated CO2 stream. Post-combustion captures CO2 from flue gases after combustion. These different types cater to various industries and offer different solutions for reducing CO2 emissions.</p></p>
