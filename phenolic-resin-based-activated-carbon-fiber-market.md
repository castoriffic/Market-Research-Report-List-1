<p><ul><li>Chemical Activation Method</li><li>Physical Activation Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Phenolic resin based activated carbon fiber is categorized into the chemical activation method and physical activation method markets. In the chemical activation method, phenolic resin is chemically treated with activating agents such as phosphoric acid or potassium hydroxide to create pores and increase the surface area for adsorption. On the other hand, the physical activation method involves heating the phenolic resin at high temperatures, causing it to decompose and form pores, resulting in a higher adsorption capacity. These different methods offer unique properties and applications for the activated carbon fiber market.</p></p>
