<p><ul><li>Rockwool Roof Insulation</li><li>Glass Wool Roof Insulation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Mineral wool roof insulation is available in two main types: rockwool and glass wool. Rockwool roof insulation is made from natural minerals, such as basalt or diabase, which are melted and then spun into fibers. It offers excellent thermal insulation properties, durability, and fire resistance. On the other hand, glass wool roof insulation is produced by melting a mixture of sand and recycled glass and then turning it into fibers. It provides good thermal and acoustic insulation and is also fire-resistant. Both types of mineral wool insulation are commonly used in the construction industry.</p></p>

<p><p>Mineral wool roof insulation is commonly used in both pitch roof insulation and flat roof insulation applications. In pitch roof insulation, mineral wool is installed between the rafters to provide thermal insulation and enhance energy efficiency. This type of insulation helps regulate the temperature inside the building and reduces heating or cooling costs. Similarly, in flat roof insulation, mineral wool is placed on top of the roof deck to prevent heat transfer and maintain a comfortable indoor environment. It offers excellent fire resistance and acoustic performance, making it an ideal choice for commercial and residential buildings.</p></p>
