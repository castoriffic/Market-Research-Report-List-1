<p><p>The industrial preservatives market includes various types such as fatty preservatives, inorganic salt preservatives, and biological preservatives. Fatty preservatives are derived from fats and oils and are used to prevent spoilage and extend the shelf life of products. Inorganic salt preservatives, like sodium benzoate and potassium sorbate, are commonly used in food and beverage industries to inhibit the growth of microorganisms. Biological preservatives, on the other hand, are derived from natural sources, such as bacteria and yeasts, and are used to prevent the growth of spoilage-causing microorganisms.</p></p>
