<p><p>In the cryogenic freezer market, Type I and Type II are two common types of freezers available. Type I cryogenic freezers are designed for long-term storage and preservation of biological samples at extremely low temperatures, typically below -150°C. These freezers offer excellent temperature stability and uniformity, making them suitable for research laboratories and biological sample banks. On the other hand, Type II cryogenic freezers are used for short-term storage and transport of samples at temperatures around -80°C. They are commonly used in medical facilities and clinics for storing vaccines, blood samples, and other temperature-sensitive pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
