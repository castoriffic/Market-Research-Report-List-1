<p><p>The biogenic pesticide market is categorized into three main types: botanical pesticide, zooid pesticide, and others. Botanical pesticides are derived from plant extracts, such as neem or pyrethrum, and are known for their low toxicity and effectiveness against various pests. Zooid pesticides, on the other hand, originate from animal sources, such as bacteria or viruses, and are commonly used for controlling insects or agricultural diseases. The "others" category includes biogenic pesticides that do not fit into the botanical or zooid classifications, such as pheromones or biochemicals. These different types provide a diverse range of options for farmers and pest control professionals to effectively manage pests while maintaining environmental sustainability.</p></p>
