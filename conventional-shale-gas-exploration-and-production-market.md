Proppants are solid particles such as sand, ceramics, resin coated sand, or other materials that are mixed with fluids and injected into shale rock formations to create fractures and allow for the release of natural gas. Sand proppants are typically cost-effective and widely used, while ceramic proppants provide better strength and conductivity. Resin coated sand offers enhanced stability and flow control. Other materials may include synthetic proppants or combinations of different substances for specific drilling conditions.</p></p>
