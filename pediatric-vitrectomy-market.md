<p><p>Pediatric vitrectomy is a surgical procedure performed on children to treat various eye conditions. Pars plana vitrectomy involves removing the vitreous gel from the eye through a small incision in the pars plana, a part of the eye located behind the iris. This procedure is commonly used to treat retinal detachments and vitreous hemorrhages in children. Anterior vitrectomy, on the other hand, involves the removal of the vitreous gel from the front part of the eye to treat conditions such as anterior segment trauma or intraocular lens implantation.</p></p>
