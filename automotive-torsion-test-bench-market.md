<p><p>The Automotive Torsion Test Bench market consists of different types based on specific parameters. Yield intensity refers to the point at which the material being tested shows permanent deformation under torsional stress. Torsional rigidity measures the ability of a material to resist torsional deformation. Shear stress refers to the force per unit area that acts parallel to the material's cross-section due to torsional load. Ductility indicates the ability of a material to undergo plastic deformation before breaking. Each of these parameters is crucial in evaluating and designing automotive parts for optimum performance and safety.</p></p>
