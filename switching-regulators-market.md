<p><p>Switching regulators are electronic devices used to convert one form of electrical energy to another with maximum efficiency. Buck regulators, also known as step-down converters, are switching regulators that lower the voltage output from a power source. They are commonly used in battery-powered devices and help to minimize power loss. Boost regulators, on the other hand, also known as step-up converters, increase the voltage output from a power source. They are used to power devices that require higher voltage levels than the available power source. Both types of switching regulators play a crucial role in various industries and applications.</p></p>
