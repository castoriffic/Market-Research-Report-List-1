<p><p>Thermoelectric generators (TEGs) are devices that convert heat energy into electrical energy, and they have two main types: multi-stage and single-stage. </p><p>Multi-stage TEGs have multiple layers of thermoelectric materials, allowing them to generate electricity from a wider range of heat sources and at higher efficiencies. They are suitable for high-temperature applications, such as power plants and industrial processes.</p><p>On the other hand, single-stage TEGs have a simpler design with only one layer of thermoelectric material. They are typically used in low-temperature applications, like waste heat recovery in vehicles and small-scale power generation.</p></p>
