<p><p>Cranial implants refer to medical devices used to correct various skull defects caused by trauma, cancer, infections, or congenital disorders. These implants are custom-made to fit the specific shape and size requirements of each patient's skull. Cranial implants can be made from materials such as titanium, polymers, ceramics, or biocompatible metals. They are placed during cranial reconstructive surgeries and play a crucial role in restoring the normal appearance and function of the skull.</p><p>The global cranial implant market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue its upward trajectory. The market is driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of skull defects and neurological disorders, advancements in implant materials and technology, rising healthcare expenditure, and growing awareness among patients and healthcare professionals.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing a shift towards personalized and minimally invasive cranial implant surgeries. With the advent of 3D printing technology, surgeons can now create patient-specific implants with high precision and accuracy. This has led to improved outcomes and reduced surgical time. Additionally, the demand for bioresorbable cranial implants is also on the rise as they eliminate the need for a second surgery to remove the implant.</p><p>Geographically, North America dominates the cranial implant market due to favorable healthcare policies, greater affordability, and the presence of key market players.
