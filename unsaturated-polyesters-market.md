<p><p>Unsaturated polyesters market is segmented into Type I and Type II. Type I includes polyesters with lower crosslink density, which are generally used in applications requiring flexibility and lower mechanical strength, such as coatings and adhesives. Type II comprises polyesters with higher crosslink density, suitable for applications demanding higher mechanical properties, such as fiberglass reinforced plastics and composites. The distinction lies in the degree of crosslinking, with Type II having a higher degree of polymer chain crosslinking compared to Type I, resulting in superior mechanical and physical properties.</p></p>
