<p><p>Automotive radar sensors play a crucial role in modern vehicles, enabling advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous driving functionalities. There are two primary types of radar sensors: CMOS and CCD. CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) radar sensors use a specific type of imaging technology that allows for more flexibility and efficient integration with other systems. On the other hand, CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) radar sensors use a different imaging technology that offers higher image quality and better light sensitivity. Both types have their advantages and are utilized in different applications within the automotive radar sensors market.</p></p>
