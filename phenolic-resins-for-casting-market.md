<p><p>Phenolic resins for casting are available in two market types: granular resin and flaky resin. Granular resin refers to a form of phenolic resin that is produced in granular or pellet-like shapes. It is commonly used in foundries for sand casting processes due to its excellent flowability and easy handling. On the other hand, flaky resin comes in flakes or thin sheet-like forms. It is primarily utilized in the shell molding process, where the resin is applied as a coating onto a refractory material, creating a shell that is hard and resistant to heat.</p></p>
