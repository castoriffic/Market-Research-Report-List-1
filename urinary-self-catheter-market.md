<p><p>The urinary self-catheter market consists of three main types: Foley catheters, intermittent catheters, and external catheters. Foley catheters are used for long-term bladder drainage and feature a balloon that inflates to keep the catheter in place. Intermittent catheters are used for short-term bladder drainage and are inserted and removed multiple times a day. External catheters, also known as condom catheters, are worn externally and are commonly used in males. Each type of catheter serves a specific purpose in managing urinary issues, providing patients with options for their specific needs.</p></p>
